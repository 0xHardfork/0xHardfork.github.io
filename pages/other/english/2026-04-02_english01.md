### Grammar Explanations

1. **Structure**: Phrasal verb "kick off"
   - **Example from dialogue**: let's kick things off
   - **Explanation**: A very common phrase in business meaning "to start" a meeting or project. (常用于商务场合，表示“开始”会议或项目。)

2. **Structure**: Phrase "waiting on"
   - **Example from dialogue**: Just waiting on QA to sign off
   - **Explanation**: "Waiting on" means depending on someone/something before you can proceed. "Sign off" means formal approval. (“waiting on”表示等待某人/某事完成才能继续。“sign off”表示正式批准。)

3. **Structure**: Phrase "second pair of eyes"
   - **Example from dialogue**: I might need a second pair of eyes on this
   - **Explanation**: Asking someone else to look at your work, usually to find a bug or error you missed. (请别人帮忙检查工作，通常是为了发现自己漏掉的错误或 Bug。)

### 文法説明 (Grammar Explanations)

1. **文法**: 〜に追われる (to be swamped/overwhelmed with)
   - **例**: 対応に追われていました
   - **説明**: 忙于处理某事，被某事追着跑的意思。

2. **文法**: 〜に引っかかる (to get caught/stuck on)
   - **例**: 私も先週それに引っかかりました
   - **説明**: 意思是在某处卡住，遇到（问题）。IT语境常指遇到bug。
