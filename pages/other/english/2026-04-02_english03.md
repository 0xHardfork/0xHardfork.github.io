### Grammar Explanations

1. **Structure**: Phrase "dead in the water"
   - **Example from dialogue**: I'm totally dead in the water here.
   - **Explanation**: Idiom meaning unable to function or make progress; completely stuck. (成语，意为无法继续工作或没有进展；彻底卡住了。)

2. **Structure**: Phrase "snap right in" / "snap into place"
   - **Example from dialogue**: Whoa, snapped right in.
   - **Explanation**: To fit or connect quickly and perfectly with a satisfying result. Often used when things suddenly work. (迅速并完美地契合或连接上。常用于形容某物突然正常工作了。)

3. **Structure**: Phrase "get it sorted"
   - **Example from dialogue**: glad we got it sorted.
   - **Explanation**: To fix a problem or organize something. (解决某个问题或整理好某事。)

### 文法説明 (Grammar Explanations)

1. **文法**: 詰んでる (completely stuck/dead in the water)
   - **例**: こっち完全に詰んでるんだ
   - **説明**: 来源于将棋。指无路可走、完全卡住或陷入死局。口语中常用于表达“没招了”。

2. **文法**: 潰していく (to rule out one by one)
   - **例**: 簡単な原因から潰していこう
   - **説明**: “溃除”。在此用来指逐步排除故障的可能性，也就是“把可能性一个接一个地打消/排除”。
