### Grammar Explanations

1. **Structure**: Idiom "throw a fit"
   - **Example from dialogue**: SonarQube is throwing a fit over some outdated packages.
   - **Explanation**: To get very angry or complain loudly. In tech, used when a tool generates many errors or refuses to proceed. (发脾气/大吵大闹。在技术语境下，指工具抛出大量报错或拒绝继续运行。)

2. **Structure**: Phrase "blow up"
   - **Example from dialogue**: Is it blowing up the whole pipeline?
   - **Explanation**: To destroy or cause to fail spectacularly. Here it means causing the build pipeline to fail completely. (炸毁/弄糟。在这里指导致整个构建流水线彻底失败。)

3. **Structure**: "Have our heads"
   - **Example from dialogue**: they'll have our heads.
   - **Explanation**: An idiom meaning someone in authority will be extremely angry and punish you severely. (字面意思是“拿走人头”，引申指领导或管理层会非常生气，狠狠训斥或处罚大家。)

### 文法説明 (Grammar Explanations)

1. **文法**: コケてる (failing/falling over)
   - **例**: CIのビルドが何度もコケてるんだけど
   - **説明**: 动词“転げる”（跌倒）的俗语形式，在IT界常被用来形容“构建失败”“程序挂掉”。

2. **文法**: ギャーギャー騒いでいる (throwing a fit/making a fuss)
   - **例**: SonarQubeがギャーギャー騒いでいる
   - **説明**: 原意是“叽叽喳喳吵个不停”，引申为系统、工具频繁地报出很多错误或警告。
